Overview

XLR8 was just over a year old when I was brought in. They had seen such rapid growth by word of mouth that the homepage had become an afterthought. However, once the initial growth ended they were in desperate need of some marketing. The comapny was founded to help build future leaders. They do this by taking chances on college students still working on their degrees. They then give these students real world responsibilities with big time clients. The result is invaluable experience for the students and an exceptional product at a fraction of the cost for the comapnies. A true win-win. XLR8 needed to convey this message in their homepage. The goal was to help potential clients and employees catch the vision of XLR8.

The Process

Like many projects, time was of the essence. Unlike many projects, I was completely on my own and the CEO did not seem to have any negative feedback. Now you may think that this was a great thing. In many ways it was. Whatever I imagined and designed was approved. I didn't have to wait for any approvals or wiggle my way through any red tape. I had complete autonomy. The fact is, their old site was so lacking that they were just happy with anything new. I was able to get the site built and deployed in time for their big campus recruiting event.

The Finished Product

So what was wrong with all this? Well, I realized that I actually missed the feedback. The whole design process was just different. The only person challenging my designs, was myself. Which created a real weird paradox. There was nothing pushing me to ask the critical questions. In the end, I do not think that I created the best possible product.The product does the job and the client was happy but I just feel like it could have been better.
