My Work

Mentors International

Overview

This project was a special one as it took my wife and I back to my former home, the Philippines. Mentors International is a global non-profit that helps people in poverty become entrepreneurs. They rely heavily on volunteers, such as my wife and I, who sacrifice their time, money and talents to further the fight against poverty. Seeing the need for a better process to track client engagement and volunteer onboarding, I set out to build a solution.

The Process

With limited time, I had to work fast. I created a team and we got to work. The process was difficult as we had limited resources and needed a solution that would work on any smartphone and ridiculously slow internet connections. We brainstormed multiple options while continuously working with management and other volunteers to collect feedback. After many iterations we were able to come to a decision. The solution was a responsive, lightweight web application built on the Django framework. Because the site would be accessed on the phone, I designed it using the mobile first methodology. Also, being the only volunteer with web development experience I had to train my team on multiple new technologies and programming languages.

Finished Product

After a very quick and agile development process, the web application was approved by upper management for deployment. By November 2018, the app will be being used in four countries, United States, Peru, Ghana and the Philippines.
